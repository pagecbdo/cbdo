Preparation of Posters and Slides

Instructions for oral presentations

  • Oral presentations will last for 15 (fifteen) minutes, with an additional 5 (five) minutes for discussion and questions
  • Slides in presentations must be written in English. Our congress has the presence of researchers from different countries, and the decision to adopt the English writing on the slides aims to facilitate the communication
  • The language of the presentation can be Portuguese, Spanish or English
Instructions for poster presentations
  • The poster supports are of tripod type, so there is no way to tape or glue the poster
  • The posters must have a rope or string to hang on the support, and tubes or rods at the top and bottom edges
  • The text of the posters must be in English
  • The maximum dimensions for posters are 90 cm (width) x 120 cm (height), equivalent to A0 size
  • Supports will be numbered according to the poster number indicated in the Book of Abstracts
  • The posters will be divided into 3 Sessions, organized by large area according to the order informed in the Book of Abstracts
  • The posters will not be displayed during the entire event, and must be hung before the respective Session and removed immediately after the end of the session
  • The virtual posters will be available for consultation on the web page. During the course of Session 2, the presenters will be able to answer questions using the virtual room at https://meet.google.com/kzb-acua-kxi

Past Colloquia

The Brazilian Colloquium on Orbital Dynamics (CBDO) has happened biannually since 1982. The first CBDO took place at the Instituto Tecnológico de Aeronáutica in July, 1982, and its organization was an initiative by Dr. Wagner Sessin. The event gathered researchers in Astronomy and Orbital Dynamics who were interested in problems of Pure and Applied Celestial Mechanics.

The first CBDO was entitled “Movimentos de Satélites Artificiais: Teoria, Determinação e Aplicações” (“Artificial Satellites Motions: Theory, Determination and Applications”) and was attended by 51 researchers and post-graduates from all over Brazil. It was followed by twenty editions of the Colloquium, four of which held at the National Institute for Space Research (INPE), the venue of the 2022 edition. Besides the impressive growth in the number of works submitted for presentation at the CBDO, the number of participants has increased and so has the geographic reach of the event, which follows the dissemination of works on Astronomy and Space Activities in our country.

The participation of researchers from other countries has been occurring since 1984, and a full list of participants can be found at http://www.astro.iag.usp.br/~dinamica/historic.htm.
